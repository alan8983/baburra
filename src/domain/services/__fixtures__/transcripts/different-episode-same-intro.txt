Hello everyone and welcome back to the show. Today we're going to be talking about some really exciting developments in the semiconductor space. But this time our focus is on Intel's turnaround strategy under Pat Gelsinger. The company has announced a major restructuring plan that involves spinning off its foundry business into a separate entity. This is a bold move that could unlock significant shareholder value if executed properly. Intel has been losing market share to AMD and TSMC for years now and this restructuring is essentially an admission that the integrated model isn't working anymore. The foundry business needs to stand on its own and compete for external customers without the legacy baggage of Intel's product divisions. Now the key question is whether Intel's eighteen A process node can actually deliver on its promises. Early test chips have shown encouraging results but there's a big difference between lab samples and high volume manufacturing. Samsung learned this lesson the hard way with their three nanometer GAA process which had terrible yields for over a year. I'm cautiously optimistic about Intel but I want to see more data before making a strong call. The risk reward at current levels is interesting but not compelling enough to go overweight. Let me also touch on AMD briefly. Lisa Su has done an incredible job turning that company around. The MI300 AI accelerator is gaining real traction in the enterprise market. While it's not going to dethrone Nvidia's H100 anytime soon, it's providing a credible alternative for customers who want to diversify their supply chain. AMD's data center revenue grew by over forty percent last quarter and I expect that momentum to continue. The stock is expensive at thirty five times forward earnings but growth companies deserve premium valuations when they're executing this well. Overall I'm neutral on Intel, bullish on AMD, and watching the foundry space very closely.
